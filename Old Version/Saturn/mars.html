OVERVIEW

Saturn Planet

Saturn is the second-largest planet in our solar system, known for its stunning ring system made of ice and rock. It primarily consists of hydrogen and helium and has over 80 moons, with Titan being the largest. Saturn's atmosphere features extreme weather patterns and strong winds. It is located about 1.2 billion kilometers from Earth. Past missions, such as Cassini-Huygens, have greatly enhanced our understanding of this gas giant and its complex features

Facts

Saturn is the second-largest planet and is mainly composed of hydrogen and helium. It has a stunning ring system, which is made of ice and rock particles. Saturn has over 80 moons, with Titan being the largest and featuring lakes of methane. It is about 1.2 billion kilometers from Earth. Notable missions include Pioneer 11, Voyager 1 and 2, and Cassini-Huygens.
