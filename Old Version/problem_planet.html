Introduction

As we seek to understand the vast universe and explore the secrets of space, the importance of studying celestial bodies, especially asteroids and comets that may pose a threat to planet Earth comes. Since the beginning of space exploration, near-Earth asteroids have posed a major scientific challenge due to the potential collision risks they may pose with our planet.

This section discusses the biggest problem in our solar system, the threat of asteroid collisions, and explores solutions to avoid any potential disaster.

1: The Problem: Asteroid collision threats with Earth

The near-Earth objects (NEOs) such as asteroids or comets pose a significant danger to Earth due to their potential to cause massive destruction. Monitoring these celestial bodies is critical to predict any risks and take preemptive action.

The Solution

Using advanced space monitoring systems, nations can track the motion of asteroids and analyze any potential risks. By leveraging advanced technology, space agencies can develop defense systems like asteroid deflection or early-warning systems to prevent possible impact.
