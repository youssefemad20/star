The Milky Way

Our Home Galaxy

Basic Identity

Type Barred Spiral
Diameter 100,000 LY
Stars 100-400 Billion
Age 13.6 Billion Years

The Milky Way is the galaxy that contains Earth, the Sun, and all stars we see in the night sky.

Structure (Important)

The Milky Way is not just a flat disk. It has:

  • Galactic Core: Dense region with a supermassive black hole (Sagittarius A*).
  • Bar: A long bridge of stars helping move gas inward.
  • Spiral Arms: Regions of star formation. We are in the Orion Arm.
  • Halo: Spherical region containing old stars and dark matter.

Why Scientists Care

  • It’s the only galaxy we can study from inside.
  • Helps understand star life cycles and planet formation.
  • Reveals Dark Matter distribution.
Most pictures of the Milky Way are artist simulations, not real photos!

Big Insight

Studying the Milky Way is like trying to understand a forest while standing inside it — difficult, but incredibly detailed.
