Andromeda (M31)

Our Cosmic Neighbor

Basic Identity

Type Spiral Galaxy
Distance 2.5M LY
Stars ~1 Trillion
Size Larger than Milky Way

Andromeda is the nearest large galaxy to us and the largest galaxy in the Local Group.

Why It Is Important

  • A reference model for spiral galaxies.
  • Close enough to observe clearly and compare to ours.
  • Helps answer: How do spiral galaxies form and age?
  • Dark Matter: Rotation speed proves dark matter exists.

The Future Collision

Yes, the Milky Way and Andromeda will collide in ~4–5 billion years.

  • Individual stars will NOT crash.
  • The galaxies will merge into "Milkomeda".
  • This helps scientists study galaxy mergers.

Big Insight

Andromeda lets us see our future and our mirror — what the Milky Way was, and what it will become.
