Scientific Deep Dive

So Far Away!

Neptune is so far from the Sun that "noon" on Neptune looks like twilight on Earth. It takes sunlight 4 hours to get there! Because it's so far out, one year on Neptune lasts 165 years on Earth.

Math Discovered It!

Neptune is the only planet that was found using math before it was seen through a telescope. Scientists noticed Uranus was moving funny and calculated that another planet must be pulling on it. They pointed their telescope where the math said, and tada! There was Neptune.

Triton: The Backward Moon

Neptune's biggest moon, Triton, orbits the planet backward! It's also super cold and shoots geysers of ice (nitrogen snow) miles high into space. It's one of the coolest (literally!) places in the solar system.
