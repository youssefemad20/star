Scientific Deep Dive

Why isn't it a Planet?

In 2006, scientists decided Pluto was too small to be a regular planet. It shares its orbit with lots of other icy rocks. But don't worry, being a Dwarf Planet is still cool! It's the King of the Kuiper Belt.

The Double Planet

Pluto's biggest moon, Charon, is almost half the size of Pluto itself! They are so close in size that they actually spin around each other. It's like they are dancing holding hands in space.

New Horizons

For a long time, Pluto was just a blurry dot. But in 2015, a spaceship called New Horizons flew past it and took amazing photos. We saw mountains made of ice, red snow, and that famous smooth heart shape made of frozen nitrogen!
