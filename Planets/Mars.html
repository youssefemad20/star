Mars

The Red Planet

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and our dream destination! It's a dusty, cold, desert world with a very thin atmosphere. It's famous for its red color, which comes from rusted iron in the ground. It's like a planet covered in rusty dust! Mars is one of the most explored bodies in our solar system, and it's the only planet where we've sent rovers to roam the alien landscape.

Scientific Deep Dive

A Mountain Higer than Everest?

Yes! Mars is home to Olympus Mons, the largest volcano in the entire solar system. It stands about three times taller than Mount Everest on Earth! It also has a giant canyon called Valles Marineris that would stretch across the entire United States.

Is there Life?

Scientists think that billions of years ago, Mars was warmer and wetter, maybe just like Earth. We send rovers (like Curiosity and Perseverance) to look for signs of ancient microscopic life in the dried-up lake beds.

Our Future Home?

NASA and other agencies want to send astronauts to Mars in the future. It's a tough trip that takes at least 6 months, and you'd have to wear a spacesuit all the time because there's no oxygen to breathe!
