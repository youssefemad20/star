Mercury

The Swift Little Planet

Meet Mercury, the smallest planet in our solar system and the closest neighbor to the Sun! It's super fast, zipping around the Sun faster than any other planet. Scientific Deep Dive

What does it look like?

Mercury looks a lot like Earth's Moon. It's gray, rocky, and covered in thousands of craters (holes made by space rocks hitting it). Since it doesn't have wind or rain to smooth them out, these craters stay there forever!

A Day Longer than a Year?

This is super weird! Mercury spins very slowly but orbits the Sun very fast. It takes 59 Earth days to spin once (a day), but only 88 Earth days to go around the Sun (a year). If you lived there, you'd have a birthday every 3 months!

Can we live there?

Probably not! It has no air to breathe and gets blasted by the Sun's radiation. But scientists are super interested in it. We've sent robot probes like MESSENGER to map its surface and learn its secrets.
