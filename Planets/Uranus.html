Scientific Deep Dive

Why is it Blue?

Uranus gets its lovely blue-green color from a gas called "methane" in its atmosphere. This gas absorbs red light and reflects blue light back to our eyes. But don't breathe it in‚Äîit would smell like rotten eggs!

Rolling Through Space

Most planets spin like spinning tops, but Uranus spins like a rolling ball. Scientists think a huge planet-sized object smashed into Uranus billions of years ago and knocked it over on its side!

Rings too!

Many people know Saturn has rings, but Uranus has them too! They aren't as bright or big, and they go up and down (vertically) instead of sideways because of the planet's tilt.
