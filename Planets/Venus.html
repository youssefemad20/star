Venus

Earth's Toxic Twin

Venus is the second planet from the Sun and the hottest planet in our solar system! Even though it's called Earth's "twin" because they are the same size, you wouldn't want to visit. It's covered in thick, yellowish clouds that trap heat like a giant blanket, making it hotter than a pizza oven! Venus is the second planet from the Sun and Earth's closest planetary neighbor. Even though Mercury is closer to the Sun, Venus is the hottest planet in our solar system because of its thick, toxic atmosphere that traps heat in a runaway greenhouse effect.

Scientific Deep Dive

Why is it so hot?

Venus has a super thick atmosphere made mostly of carbon dioxide. This creates a "Greenhouse Effect" gone wild! It lets the Sun's heat in but won't let it escape. That's why Venus is even hotter than Mercury, even though Mercury is closer to the Sun.

The Backward Planet

Venus is a rebel! Most planets spin the same way (counter-clockwise), but Venus spins backward (clockwise). On Venus, the Sun rises in the west and sets in the east. It also spins super slowly.

The Morning Star

Because Venus' thick clouds reflect sunlight like a mirror, it looks super bright in our sky. Often, it's the first "star" you see after sunset or the last one before sunrise. That's why people call it the "Morning Star" or "Evening Star".
